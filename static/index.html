The five screening tests
1
Business activity
Core operations must be halal
The company's primary revenue must come from Shariah-permissible activities. Banks, conventional insurance firms, alcohol, tobacco, weapons, and adult entertainment companies are blocked outright.
Threshold: Binary (block / allow)
2
Debt ratio
Interest-bearing debt to total assets
Measures the proportion of financing from Riba-based (interest-bearing) sources. High debt ratios indicate heavy reliance on conventional finance which violates Islamic principles.
Threshold: Interest-bearing debt / Total assets < 37%
3
Investment ratio
Non-compliant investments to total assets
Investments in non-compliant enterprises (conventional banks, haram industries) must stay below the threshold. This includes equity holdings in prohibited companies.
Threshold: NC investments / Total assets < 33%
4
Income ratio
Non-compliant income to total revenue
Interest income, late-payment charges, and other Riba-tainted income as a proportion of revenue. Compliant companies must give this income to charity (purification). This is what the purification calculator computes.
Threshold: NC income / Total revenue < 5%
5
Illiquid assets ratio
Tangible assets must exceed one-quarter of total assets
Ensures the company has a sufficient base of tangible, illiquid assets (property, plant, equipment, inventories). Predominantly liquid companies resemble money-trading (Bai al-dayn) and must pass the net-liquid-assets per share test instead.
Threshold: Illiquid assets / Total assets ≥ 25%

Screening Methodology

Overview

Sharia Scope applies the AAOIFI (Accounting and Auditing Organisation for Islamic Financial Institutions) Shariah Standard No. 21 screening criteria, as adopted by the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) for its KMI-30 index.

The five quantitative tests are computed from raw balance-sheet and income-statement data. No pre-approved company list is consulted — the verdict is calculated purely from the numbers you supply.

The Five Tests

#TestFormulaThreshold
1Business activityQualitativeCore ops must be halal; banks, alcohol, weapons, etc. are blocked
2Debt ratioIBD ÷ Total assets × 100< 37%
3Investment ratioNC investments ÷ Total assets × 100< 33%
4Income ratioNC income ÷ Total revenue × 100< 5%
5Illiquid assets ratioIlliquid assets ÷ Total assets × 100≥ 25%

Net Liquid Assets per Share

When a company fails the illiquid assets test (≥ 25%), it may still be compliant if its net liquid assets per share exceed its current market price. This mirrors the KMI screening rule for asset-heavy-but-liquid firms.

Formula: NLA/share = (Total assets − Illiquid assets − Total liabilities) ÷ Shares outstanding

Compare NLA/share against the market price per share.

Purification

For compliant companies where non-compliant income is > 0%, shareholders must donate a proportionate amount of their dividends to charity. This purifies the investment.

Formula: Purification per share = Dividend per share × (NC income ÷ Total revenue)

Data Notes

  • All financial inputs should be in the same unit (e.g., all in PKR millions). Only the ratios matter, so the unit cancels out.
  • Interest-bearing debt includes bank borrowings, debentures, lease liabilities, and any other Riba-based financing.
  • Illiquid assets = PPE + intangibles + long-term investments + inventories (tangible assets not readily convertible to cash).
  • Non-compliant income includes interest received, late-payment penalties, and income from prohibited subsidiaries.

Glossary

Riba
Unjustified increase in exchange or loan transactions. In modern finance this includes interest on loans and deposits. Forbidden by the Quran (2:275–276).
Source: AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 21
Bai al-dayn
Sale of debt. Selling predominantly cash/receivable assets at a premium or discount is prohibited. This is why the illiquid assets test exists — companies whose assets are mostly cash resemble money-selling.
Source: AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 21
AAOIFI
Accounting and Auditing Organisation for Islamic Financial Institutions. A Bahrain-based international autonomous non-profit body that issues Shariah, accounting, auditing, ethics, and governance standards for Islamic financial institutions.
aaoifi.com
KMI-30
KMI-30 (Karachi Meezan Index) is Pakistan's first and premier Shariah-compliant index, tracking 30 companies screened by the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) using AAOIFI criteria reviewed semi-annually.
psx.com.pk
Purification
When a Shariah-compliant company earns a small amount of prohibited income (e.g. bank interest on overnight deposits), investors must donate the proportional share of their dividends to charity to "purify" the investment.
AAOIFI Standard No. 21, clause 3
Interest-bearing debt (IBD)
Financing that carries a fixed or variable interest obligation: bank loans, overdrafts, conventional bonds (debentures), and finance lease liabilities. Islamic sukuk are excluded.
PSX KMI screening guidelines
Illiquid assets
Property, plant and equipment; intangible assets; long-term investments in non-cash instruments; inventories. Assets not immediately convertible to cash without a significant loss in value.
AAOIFI Standard No. 21
Net Liquid Assets per Share (NLA/share)
A secondary test for companies with low illiquid assets. NLA/share = (Total assets − Illiquid assets − Total liabilities) ÷ Shares outstanding. If NLA/share < market price, the shares trade at a premium to cash — which resembles Bai al-dayn.
PSX KMI screening guidelines
